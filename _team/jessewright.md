Jesse Wright is a PhD student in Computer Science at the University of Oxford, specializing in decentralized web architectures and AI. As a Technical Advisor at Doctelligence, he leverages his extensive experience with decentralized systems to guide the development of scalable, secure, and efficient decentralized AI solutions.

Appointed by Sir Tim Berners-Lee, founder of the World Wide Web, Jesse leads the technical development of Solid, an open-source decentralized web project designed to give individuals full control over their data. This experience with managing and scaling complex decentralized systems directly informs his work at Doctelligence, where he helps shape the technical infrastructure of the Decentralized Intelligence Network (DIN).

Jesse’s work bridges AI and decentralized systems, helping advance frameworks for decentralized, peer-to-peer networks as part of the Ethical Web and Data Architectures Group (EWADA) at Oxford. His expertise in integrating AI technologies with decentralized networks positions him to contribute significantly to Doctelligence’s mission to develop an open-source AI ecosystem.

By leveraging his technical knowledge, Jesse ensures that Doctelligence’s DIN network remains scalable and adaptable to meet the demands of the evolving decentralized AI landscape. His leadership optimizes both the architecture and technical skills needed to deliver a high-performance, user-centric, and secure system.
