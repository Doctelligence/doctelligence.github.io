**Abraham Nash** is a **Decentralized AI Researcher** at the University of Oxford, where he is pursuing a **PhD** in **Human-Centered Computing**. His research focuses on **decentralized systems**, **data sovereignty**, and **blockchain**, with a goal to create more equitable, user-centric digital ecosystems through decentralized AI (DeAI).

Abraham initially **graduated with a medical degree** from the **University of London** before shifting his focus to technology and AI. He later earned a **Master’s degree** from the **University of Cambridge**, where he developed **AI tools** for real-world applications. His academic journey spans medicine, AI development, and systems architecture, providing a unique interdisciplinary approach to building decentralized systems that prioritize individual data sovereignty.

As the **author of the vision paper** for the **Decentralized Intelligence Network (DIN)**, Abraham is helping shape the future of AI by promoting decentralization and user control over data. His work at **Doctelligence** aims to empower individuals to control and monetize their data while contributing to the development of decentralized AI models.

With a strong foundation in technology and systems architecture, Abraham is committed to advancing AI in a way that empowers individuals and ensures technologies serve the public good.
