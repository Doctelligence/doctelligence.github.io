Abraham Nash is a PhD student in Computer Science at the University of Oxford. His research focuses on decentralized systems, data sovereignty, and blockchain, with the aim of efficiently utilizing data for AI development through decentralized AI (DeAI).

Abraham began his academic journey with a background in technology before transitioning to AI research. He later earned a Master's degree from the University of Cambridge, where he developed AI tools for real-world applications. Now a PhD student at the University of Oxford, his research focuses on decentralized systems, blockchain, and AI architectures. This interdisciplinary approach equips him with a broad skill set for building decentralized systems that prioritize data sovereignty and efficient AI development.

As the author of the vision paper for the Decentralized Intelligence Network (DIN), Abraham is helping shape the future of AI by promoting decentralization and user control over data. His work at Doctelligence aims to empower individuals to control and monetize their data while contributing to the development of decentralized AI models.

With a strong foundation in technology and systems architecture, Abraham is committed to advancing AI in a way that empowers individuals and ensures technologies serve the public good.
